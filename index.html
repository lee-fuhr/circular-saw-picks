How we choose our picks

We don’t run our own timed cuts or torque tests. Instead, we base every recommendation on a careful synthesis of three sources: long-term owner feedback from forums and review archives, published expert reviews from trusted trade publications and YouTube channels, and our own notes from watching what actually breaks (or doesn’t) on worksites and in basements. We prioritize models that have proven their durability over years, not just out of the box. A saw that slips in bevel adjustment after six months or burns through brushes in a busy season gets dinged hard. We also look for honest trade-offs, a lighter saw may wobble more under load, or a budget model may have poor dust port alignment. Our goal is to tell you what a saw is like to live with, not just to unbox.
