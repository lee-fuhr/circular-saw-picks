Best budget circular saws for 2026

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The Bosch CS10 is our top budget pick because it delivers smooth, reliable cuts at a very affordable wired price.

The Milwaukee 2630-20 is the best premium cordless option for pros who want maximum power, while the Ryobi P508 is the ideal choice for DIYers on a tight cordless budget.

A good circular saw doesn’t have to cost a fortune. If you are doing occasional home projects or even regular job-site work, there are plenty of budget-friendly models that cut cleanly and hold up over time. The trick is knowing where to compromise: corded saws give you consistent power for less money, while cordless saws trade runtime for convenience. Here we look at three saws that each excel at a different price point, so you can find the right balance of affordability, performance, and features for your needs. When choosing a budget circular saw, focus on three things: motor power (amp rating or voltage), depth of cut, and bevel capacity. A saw with a 15-amp motor and a 2-1/2-inch cut depth at 90 degrees will handle nearly everything a homeowner or light contractor throws at it. Also check the quality of the included blade, budget saws often come with a mediocre blade that you may want to upgrade. And don’t overlook the baseplate: a stamped steel base is typical at this price, but a heavier aluminum base can keep cuts true over time.
